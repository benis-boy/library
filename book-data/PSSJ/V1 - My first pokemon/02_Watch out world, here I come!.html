The ever sour hero sluggishly packs up and leaves, hoping to Arceus to get his starter pokemon soon.

But Arceus does not hear his prayers. Or ignored them.

Because the days pass and the increasingly depressed hero marches on in a funk.

Waking up more and more tired, getting more and more sick of the taste of Aspear and Oran Berry.

But also getting more and more calloused to the brutality of the wild world of pokemon.

On the bright side, his body is getting fitter and fitter every day too, thanks to hours of hiking and due to his strict diet of healthy berries, lean protein and hard to digest carbs.

He has also lost the last vestiges of body fat, turning him spindle-thin, like some kind of gangly ghoul.

Another thing he is getting used to, is to listen to the shouts and cries of wild pokemon.

He has watched so many Starly hunt in their groups, that he has learned to tell what sounds they make when they find a new prey, when they use a Wing Attack or Quick Attack and he learned the patterns they use in their unfair fighting style.

Unfair, but effective.

In the many pokemon fights Swole has watched, not once did a single pokemon beat the teamwork of the cowardly creatures.

One Starly would distract and then the other punishes their prey as a perfect duo team.

Rattata are similar. They would overwhelm their target with hordes of five. Even a stronger target would stand no chance against their relentless assault.

Some bug pokemon would wait for an unsuspecting pokemon to wander into their nets, then pelter it from above with poison attacks. He almost stepped into one of these traps but noticed in time due to his constant vigilance and paranoia.

The young hero might not notice it now, but the hardships he lives through these days are giving him the lessons he needs to become truly great some day.

His will may falter on some mornings. Sometimes he just wants to give up and go home; But his desire to become a champion reigns supreme.

...

...

...

And so the days rush past him in a blur for almost two weeks. On the 14th day of his journey - when our young hero was at his very lowest... He gave up his initial strategy.

The words of his father jumped to his mind: "I brawled with him."

Our hero has spent that day thinking about what pokemon he could ever beat up in a fair fight.

And as luck would have it, near sunset, he finds a Bidoof!

He is fat, hunched over something in the distance.

While watching him from the safety of the thicket Swole worked up his courage to challenge him to a battle.

Slowly, but with plenty of determination Swole made his way out of the thicket and closer to Bidoof.

... Until he saw what Bidoof was hunched over. Rattata corpses.

A whole horde of dead Rattata. At least 5.

Swole was unable to count properly because of the distance and because some of them were ripped into pieces while others had already been mostly eaten.

He freezes in the middle of his step. Blood drains out of his face together with all the collected courage.

In our hero's mind, this should not have happened. Ever.

A Bidoof, the most pudgy, useless and weakest pokemon, right after Magikarp, should not be munching on bones like they were fries.

Slowly he steps back. He was sure that he did not make any noise but Bidoof had noticed something.

He stops chewing a Rattata leg and slowly waddles around on his fat, stumpy, little legs.

Swole sees his blood covered snout and freezes up completely.

The empty gaze in Bidoof's eyes examines the bushes of the thicket.

Searching for even more food but he does not find anything. Bidoof brushes the distraction off his slow mind and continues his disturbing feast.

Swole stays frozen. What Swole saw were not the eyes of a cute, little, widdle Bidoof.

Those were the eyes of a seasoned murderer. How many innocent pokemon did this creature devour? Judging by how fat Bidoof was, too many.

The hero spent the 14th evening listening to the sounds of bones being crushed by a chubby Bidoof.

"Fighting pokemon on my own was a dumb idea anyway." Thought the hero to himself as Bidoof bites through another Rattata leg. "... I didn't want a Bidoof in my team either."

Only after nightfall did Bidoof finally fall asleep in the middle of the gore and our hero spent that whole night running as far away from Bidoof as possible.
