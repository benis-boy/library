Blood and tears boil within the empty sockets of a lifeless husk. Cracked and charred skin stretches taut over his bones. The blistering winds whipping at his back finally find a crevice large enough to dig in.

He trembles. He groans. For the first time in hours, the pain overwhelms him as his skin tears. Like wings made of flesh, they open up, exposing his ribs, his spine, and what's left of his muscles to the constant hail of rocks and shards of bone.

He would scream if he still could. He yearns for his body to recover just to curse God again and again for forsaking him - sentencing him to hell with the rest of the filthy sinners.

All around him, people are nailed to the ground in various states of their skin melting, burning, or tearing. Only a few did not yet tear their vocal cords and scream. Thick metal rods puncture their wrists, knees, and feet, forcing them to prostrate themselves before the demons.

Terrible creatures with leathery wings and pretty faces. Their bodies range from lascivious to grotesque with reverted joints or inverted skin. One of whom kicks the broken man in the face with her cloven foot.

"Wakey wakey, Gustave." She giggles gleefully. Her face is angular and sharp with not a hint of age showing on her youthful visage. Her red lips would almost look inviting if they weren't covered in blood. Just past her neck, the facade of a face gives way to her real shape - that of a slender, skinless woman with the legs of a goat. "Can you hear me? Grunt once if you can."

He doesn't. So she stomps on his head, grinding her hooves into the back of his skull until he chokes on the ashes covering the floor. Still hearing nothing, she changes her target, kicking his open back instead, hard enough to crack some bones and force the air from his lungs.

"There we go. One grunt. I knew you could hear me, little wrathling." Pleased with herself, she takes a seat on his prostrate form, using his calves as her footrest and his shoulder blade as the armrest when she turns to keep talking with him. "Do you know why I'm here today?"

She doesn't give him a pause to reply. She tucks a few wayward strands of hair behind her horns and continues, "No, it's not because you're my favorite sinner. Don't be ridiculous. I'm a professional. I don't do favorites." She leans closer and digs her razor-sharp fingernails into his flesh. "I lied. You're my favorite. Tehe."

Her trill of a laugh, her haughty voice, and the hot breath caressing his ears would fill any blind man with lust, if it weren't for the excruciating pain frying his brain at any given moment.

"But for real, today is a special day. We're having a cool new event to commemorate all the newlings."

As if awaiting her announcement, the wind suddenly stops. The air no longer burns with the heat of an inferno, and the hail of rocks and bones trickles to a slow stop as the last pieces fall to earth and sink into the layer of ashes.

"It's the first change of scenery in millennia! I'm so excited! Are you excited, Gustave? And look! We're giving you a fresh, fully healed body to enjoy it with, isn't that awesome?!"

Again, along with her words come the changes. His flesh stitches itself back together, and his burns heal. His eyeballs regrow, then his skin turns white and fills with fresh blood. The scent of sulfur immediately stings in his nose the moment the nerves for it are restored. And finally, when his hair regrows, the demon wastes not a moment before digging her fingers into his scalp to lift his head, pulling strong enough to look in his eyes while still sitting on his butt.

"First: the formalities. You probably know the drill by now, or maybe you forgot? Can you tell me what I'm about to tell you?"

Gustave grits his teeth. "Piss off, foul demon." His eyes widen in surprise when he hears his own voice. How long has it been since he used it for anything besides screaming in pain?

The demoness just laughs it off. "Oh, Gustave! Still filled with so much fury. It's adorable! You've barely changed a bit in those 750 years since you came down here for your punishment. Ah!" She holds up a finger - cutely - as if remembering something. She hops up from her perch before hurrying to stand in front of Gustave with a piece of parchment she pulled from thin air.

"Right. Ahem! Brother Gustave de Payns, Temple Knight of Champagne - for your crimes against humanity, God's creation, you are sentenced to hell. For the indiscriminate slaughter of enemies long after being ordered to show mercy, your sin is wrath. You are to burn until your soul turns to ashes and the Lord can make you anew."

Despite having heard that for the hundredth or thousandth time, today marks the first his mind can really listen to them. He can finally remember the sins he committed before his untimely demise - remember the faces of the bastards he wiped out - remember what they did to deserve it.

A crazed look crosses his eyes as he lifts his head to stare her dead in the eyes. "I would do it again."

It's these words that cause the demoness to prance around and jump while clapping her hands and letting a bleating giggle escape. Her hooves now clop against an asphalt street before she calms herself and squats down in front of Gustave with her knees spread far apart. A few fingers caress his face. "That's why you're my favorite! So unrepentant! So stoic! Only someone who truly believes in their sins can be like this."

It would be quite arousing if a demon's caress didn't involve her claws digging through Gustave's flesh down to the bones on his jaw. He can only scream in pain until she lets him go and the wounds quickly heal themselves.

"You know?" The demoness shuffles around uncomfortably. A blush spreads across her face. "Since you are my favorite, I can get you a place in the front row for the show alongside the other big shots like Alexander and Joseph."

"I would rather not."

"Muhammad is also here just for today. Tomorrow it's back to being sliced thinly for him. I'm sure you have a lot to say to him, being a Templar and all that."

Before he can reply, she continues. "Great! Then it's decided! Let me just pull these out--" His screams echo through a strange forest of concrete and glass - buildings as tall as the eye can see, repeating in every direction. With a metallic clang, the last rod pinning him to the floor falls aside before sinking through the asphalt street.

Then, with the snap of a finger, Gustave's surroundings change. Barbed wire now binds him to a chain-link fence. It loops around his neck, his arms from the wrists up to the shoulder, and his legs too.

The sharp pain causes him to scream his lungs out until his body settles and hangs within the wire mesh with his wounds already fusing shut around the barbs. Shifting his head pains him, but he looks around nonetheless.

To his left: Alexander the Great.

And to his right, "You! You filthy bastard! Apostate! Burn in hell!"

Despite the irony, his neighbor refuses to respond. He's left in a catatonic state, unable to blink, unable to think. Just waiting for the punishment to resume.

"Dirty, disgusting liar! I'll kill-- Ahhhh!" His cursing was cut short when the demoness assigned to him gently pats his cheek while fondling his chest sensually.

"There, there." She chirps as she plays around with a loose strand of his pectoral muscles. "I know he riles you up easily, but let's focus on the show, alright?" She finishes up by tightening her grip around his jaw before planting a kiss on his lips.

"Malice?" A deep grumbling voice from a grotesquely malformed creature rings out behind her. "Is that you? What are you doing here? Did you get a raise?"

Malice, the demoness with surprisingly innocent and pretty eyes, rolls them in their sockets after hearing the voice. "Urgh. No, Chadwick the Shackler. Go away. Can't you see I'm here with my human?"

"Whore." He grunts before turning away and dragging the heavy iron chains with him that are meant to keep the flighty traitors locked in place.

"Sorry you had to see that, Gustave." His only acknowledgment of her concern is him thrashing and screaming in pain as her claws reach around his teeth to fondle his tongue. "And it looks like I've wasted a little too much time. The show is about to begin. Take a look around."

She retracts her fingers from his body, allowing the wounds to quickly fill up while Gustave looks at the strange sight in front of him.

Metal constructs he has never seen before lie scattered in front of him with coarse sand covering the floor. The only clue he gets is children playing around - climbing all over the constructs, swinging back and forth with them. All of them have an emptiness in their eyes that makes them seem closer to flesh dolls than people.

One of them lingers a little off to the side, standing out by the way he's fixated on something in the sky. Tracing his sight, Gustave spots a lonely bird among the clouds, making a humming noise as it glides through the air.

It releases something from its belly, reminding Gustave of bird droppings, but the scale of these things doesn't make any sense to him.

"Welcome to the 20th century." Malice tears him from his observations by poking through his ribs. "Humanity has been busy as a bee while you were stuck down here. Meaning we don't have to bore you any longer with these wildfires that whip across ancient battlefields. Take a deep breath. Enjoy the moment. Because today is a brand new day in hell."

At her words, the sun suddenly rises underneath the ashen skies of hell.

The very next moment, Gustave's eyes burst, his skin boils and tears as his blood turns into steam. He never even had the time to scream before his throat clamped shut under the pressure of being cooked alive.

The pressure wave hits next, shattering his bones and pushing his liquefying body partway through the chain-link fence.

Any human would have died instantly under the intense heat of the nuclear bomb, but the damned are not given this privilege. His flesh regrows as fast as it turns to ashes. His eyes fill up once more to grant but a flash of sight before they pop like eggs in a microwave. His lungs reform, but his body can't breathe the air he so desperately desires before the necessary nerves become undone.

Despite the heat, he feels as if drowning. Not a single conscious thought pierces through the veil of panic, pain, and distress that would turn any man insane.

That lasts for days. Perhaps for years? Or just moments? Gustave can't tell, nor can he even wonder about it. His mind, unraveled and made whole again and again, is frying in the blazing sun.

But then, calm.

His body heals in a mere minute. His eyes are drawn as if his broken mind works on its own. When they behold him in his sight, he understands.

An angel has arrived, casting his protective shadow over him.

He's a faceless man with a head full of long, golden hair. His locks reach down to the shoulders, never straying from their place framing the featureless, blurry skin. Somehow, Gustave can tell the man is handsome before even a single thought has managed to form in his mind.

Gustave can't take him out of his sight; at the edge of his vision, his eyes barely make out a piece of parchment held in one of the angel's hands. "A temple knight? However did you manage to find yourself at the very center of this hell? Were you fighting the demons to escape?"

Gustave wants to reply - his soul commands him, yet his body can only let out a string of nonsensical babble. "Uwoa." But suddenly, his eyes widen. His mouth, tongue, lungs, throat, and vocal cords move on their own as if guided by providence. His voice comes out clear and filled with admiration. "Michael."

"Indeed." Archangel Michael. "It pleases me to see that there is still a spark of a soul left in you, Gustave de Payns. Look around yourself."

Gustave cannot disobey the command, no matter how much he desires to stare at the beautiful being hovering in front of him.

Every way he looks, destruction is all he finds. The chain-link fence still holds, yet he's alone - the only one who hangs bound by the barbed wires.

Winds, faster than anything he's ever seen, whip up metal shrapnel, shards of glass, concrete debris the size of boulders, and fire. All burns. The playground in front of him has turned flat. The constructs have disappeared into the ground, and the once happy children are nothing but shadows among the clutter.

The skyscrapers in the distance have toppled. Tortured souls still burn within their wreckages.

And just a few hundred meters behind Michael's head, the sun still blazes. Yet the halo - proof of his holy nature to any creature dumb enough not to tell at a glance - outshines it still.

His spread wings eclipse the sun and cast a shadow, yet Gustave can still see just fine underneath his divinity. He understands now where the winged Hussars pulled their inspiration from; however, the imitations pale in comparison.

"How does it make you feel?"

The question overwhelms him, loaded with meaning beyond his comprehension. Tearing free concepts that his mind cannot shape anymore, he simply mutters the first word that reaches his tongue. "F-feel?"

Michael grins. He can't see his lips, but he understands it nonetheless. "Yes, Gustave. Doesn't it fill you with pride to be the last one standing? To be God's last warrior?"

"H-his last?"

"His very last. Most reached heaven, others succumbed to the temptation of surrender. All have perished. All are remade. But not you." Suddenly, a sword appears held in his hand. From one moment to the next, it began to exist. "Gustave de Payens - Templar Knight hailing from Champagne - for slaughtering entire families and those who surrendered with this very sword, your rightful punishment is to burn in hell until your soul is nothing but ashes."

The words fall like a warhammer to Gustave's beaten pride. And just like the words, the sword falls as well; past Michael's shadow, the pommel glints in the baleful sun's glare as it pierces the earth.

"This sword was your undoing, and now it shall be your salvation. Grasp it, my undying warrior, and return. The world needs the likes of you."

"B--"

"But!? Have I misjudged you?!" His booming voice echoes through hell louder than the everlasting nuclear blast. "Are you not a soldier of God?! Will you refuse the call even when I scream it?!"

His ears bleed. Bloodshot eyes stare at the being in front of him as his heavy head slowly shakes left and right.

"Speak up! Your sin is wrath, is it not?! Show me your fervor! Show me the devotion of a being so filled with love and hate!"

"I will not refuse the call! I will always fight for God! I will never surrender!"

"Louder!"

The command busts every vessel in Gustave's body at once. Blood streams from his eyes, his ears, and even his mouth.

He chokes just once while taking a deep breath of air that is hot enough to singe his lungs. "I AM GOD'S BLADE! I AM HIS SHIELD!"

"Good." All at once, Gustave's body has been restored. "Grasp it, and you shall return. Bring righteous judgment to the damned or become a martyr yet again."

A scale appears in his other hand with one side hanging much lower than the other. Scripture in an unknown language is written on the front; the moment Gustave sees it, he understands that it's his name.

"Heaven or hell. Mercy or cruelty. The choice is yours."

Then, without another word, Archangel Michael disappears.

The nuclear blast resumes with a vengeance, popping Gustave's eyes like grapes yet again and melting his flesh in nearly an instant.

However, the time he had before his limbs shut down was just enough to move his shoulder a little less than an inch. The moment his body resets, he moves it a little more than one. Barbed wire digs deep into his flesh, wrapping around his bones in some places - still, he pulls.

With every chance he gets, he tears his flesh further. With every flash of sight, the glinting pommel just meters in front of his eyes shines a little brighter. With every death of his body, a wicked visage burns into his face - smiling without lips. Crying without tears. A jaw frozen in a scream that he cannot release.

A devil of wrath.

All around him, infernal creatures emerge from their hiding, digging themselves free from their forts made from abandoned shopping carts or marching through the molten streets of a burning wasteland, drawn to Gustave like curious fawns.

One strays too close. His clawed hand hisses, shrivels, and burns up before his eyes, turning into ashes carried off by the winds. The creature screams, staring at the stump of an arm as if the screech could regrow the bones, flesh, and skin.

Another pushes the demon ahead, making him stumble off the wreckage he stood upon and fall into his death. Squealing like a pig at slaughter, writhing on the ground like a rabid beast, popping, hissing, and bursting like a sausage on a grill, the demon dies.

They all stare with terror etched on their faces, wearily watching their neighbors who could fling them to their doom at any moment. Parts of their minds know they should retreat - they can't. Something pulls them closer.

"It's the sword." A demon hisses, inaudible over the rushing winds.

Another repeats it. "The sword!" He has a human's head on top of a mantis's body with 16 legs.

Then another demon shouts it out loud. "It's the sword!"

More and more utter and mumble. Hundreds of them join the mad choir atop piles of flattened buildings. Just as the swarm is about to reach their crescendo, one whispers something new. "What do we do?"

"What does it mean?"

"A divine sword."

"I want it!"

"It's mine!"

A small creature with horns and wings and gnarled bones protruding from his joints suddenly lunges for the artifact. His body frays at the edges as it burns, and long before he could touch the sword, he puffs into a cloud of ashes, torn apart by the nuclear storm.

Those who had approached closer in their frenzy all take a step back. A particularly large demon, the shape of an ancient tree with limbs of bark and burning moss for wings, arrives from the older parts of hell, reigniting their bloodlust with his grumbled words, "It's a key. A way to escape."

Larger and dumber creatures throw themselves to their doom. The shining beacon that lures them with freedom is not meant for them.

"Ahhh--!" A human's brief scream cuts through the storm, halting their suicidal onslaught.

Gustave has freed an arm. Strips of skin remain in the barbed wire mesh.

Pointing and gasping, a skinless demoness with horns and the bottom half of a goat bleats, "He's trying to escape!"

"Get him!" Another one shouts but doesn't dare to act on his own. Nobody who's dumb enough remains. "Don't you know what will happen to us if he leaves?!"

"Lucifer..."

"He'll kill us all!"

"So will this! We can't stop it!"

"Whose human is it?!"

Malice shrinks into herself, trying not to be seen while some creature pulls out a stack of parchment from thin air.

"It says right here, he's Gustave de Payens!" A few creatures murmur in recognition after hearing the famous family's name. "And his punishment was signed off by... Malice!"

It doesn't take long for eyes to land on her huddled form. She tries to get up and run, but a powerful hand grabs her by the shoulder, crushing it in his grasp.

"This is your fault!" He hisses with a forked tongue while she shakes her head wildly. His lower half is a snake that slithers as his human torso lifts her up. Malice's sharp claws uselessly tear at his scaled arm, breaking and chipping off piece by piece until her fingers bleed. Her powerful legs buck, striking him in the face with her cloven hooves, but he shrugs of the pain and spits out a long, poison-filled tooth.

Malice screams, bleats like a goat, and pleads for her life. Despite it, the monstrosity flings her from the small hill of rubble they stood upon. She rolls down the slope, desperately flailing and screeching until her back breaks through a crust of glass, its shards digging between her ribs.

Her breathing hitches. She fell into the former playground, the new sun blazing directly overhead. Her long and cushy life of torturing damned souls flashes before her eyes as she writhes expecting her true death to be painful... yet nothing comes for her.

"What?!" The demon that flung her shouts in frustration. He chases her and slithers down his hill a little too fast. He cannot stop when his nose turns to dust. His face and lips follow, then his eyes as he breaks into a stumble. Like a specter of smoke, he leaves a trail behind that's quickly whisked away by the constant storm.

At the end, he's nothing but a loose bundle of brittle bones - a dusty tumbleweed that smacks into Malice.

An ash-coated smile spreads on her lips as her demonic allies jeer and demand her to die. She pulls herself from the ground, clutching her shoulder and flipping off the viewers before stomping on the snake demon's skull, crushing it to dust underfoot.

The hollers grow louder and frantic as she turns away. She slowly stalks across the field of glass and molten children's toys. She steps across the shadow of a child, frozen in its final scream, uncaring for the footprint she leaves behind. A singular goal glints under her gaze.

The sword. Its radiance is like flame to a moth.

She approaches, trying her best to ignore the growls of a mindless animal. Gustave lets out another groan as his second arm is pulled free from its barbed prison. "Mine--" He sputters before the heat boils the nerves between the brain and his mouth.

Malice glances between the blade and its monstrous owner. A being of wrath, of molten flesh and boiling blood, hangs slumped in the chainlink fence with his legs still bound and his back burning under the sun.

A fallen Templar Knight. Her precious, darling wrathling. "I would never leave behind what is mine." A broken claw pierces through the soft parts behind his chin and bumps against his teeth, lifting his head so their eyes can meet. Once again, they rupture, but she knows he has seen that brief flash of her face. "You will let me help, won’t you?"

She has the faux decency to ask before tearing at his bare legs, uncaring about the pain and suffering she causes as she strips the flesh from his bones. "Quite the tangle you got there." Not even she can tear apart the barbed wires of his prison, so she has no choice but to slowly release the knots and loops around his bone, constantly flaying his regenerating body to reach them.

After taking half an eternity to finish the task, she judges it to be too tedious and simply tears at the second leg with all her might. Crushing his bones in the endeavor, his final limb slips free only for Gustave to flop to the ground like a dead fish.

It doesn't take him long before he resumes moving, clawing at the glass floor. Inch by inch with every chance he gets.

Malice watches him squirm. She shudders in delight, almost losing track of time when he reaches for the blade still way too far away. "Don't worry, I can help." A sickly sweet smile caresses her lips as she takes his hand. Chipped claws dig into the back of his, pressing and wiggling until they pierce and slide between his bones.

She wears his hand like a deranged glove, pulling his whole body along until she grabs the sword handle.

In a brilliant flash of light, the mortal ream's fate has changed.
